Before DNS

Before the Domain Name System (or DNS for short) was invented, people had to manually add to their HOSTS.TXT file.

Elizabeth Feinler maintained the first address directory at the Network Information Center. People had to contact the center to manually add to their own HOSTS.TXT file. Her team also developed first concept of domains, with the idea of names like .edu floating in the air.

The Birth

Paul Mockapetris replaced the manual operation of looking up and mapping addresses with an automated distributed database, mapping domain names to addresses (example.com -> 64.6.98.185).

Symbolics.com became the first (and longest) ever domain to be registered.
